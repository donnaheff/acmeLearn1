# AcmeLearn launch usability study

## Participants
Recruit 8–10 prospective IELTS learners in Nigeria across mobile and desktop, including at least two keyboard/screen-reader users. Do not use existing staff. Obtain research consent and avoid collecting exam documents or payment details.

## Critical journeys
1. Arrive on homepage, understand the offer and begin the diagnostic.
2. Register with email and with one social provider.
3. Compare plans and complete a sandbox Paystack checkout.
4. Find and join a test Zoom lecture as an enrolled learner.
5. Complete a writing draft, reconnect after simulated network loss and submit.
6. Locate tutor feedback and update notification preferences.
7. Export account data and find account deletion controls.

## Success criteria
- 90% complete each core journey without moderator intervention.
- Median diagnostic discovery under 30 seconds.
- No participant sees a meeting link without enrolment.
- No draft loss after refresh or reconnect.
- System Usability Scale target: 80+.
- Zero WCAG 2.2 AA critical issues.

## Capture
Use `research.html` for ratings and comments. Record task completion, time, hesitation, error and severity. Never record a participant’s password, payment card, private Zoom URL or speaking sample.

## Release rule
Block production launch for any authorization bypass, payment/enrolment mismatch, lost assessment response, inaccessible core control or severity-1 privacy defect. Track lower-severity findings in a dated remediation backlog.
